Rubber Sheet Simulation: Potential Surface φ(x,y)
Metaphor Guide Image
Main Learning Goal

Understand principle → find parameter relationships → establish equations

Principle: source distribution creates curvature in the potential surface.

Relationship: charge/source strength \(\rho\), medium stiffness/permittivity \(\epsilon\), spread \(\sigma\), sign, and position determine \(\phi\), \(\nabla^2\phi\), and \(E\).

Equation: students should build toward \(\nabla^2\phi=-\rho/\epsilon\) and \(E=-\nabla\phi\), not just memorize them.

Learning Cycle

Predict → Test → Explain → Ask AI → Revise

Before changing the simulation, students should make a prediction. After the plot updates, they explain the result using the equation.

Core equations: \(\nabla^2\phi=-\rho/\epsilon\), \(E=-\nabla\phi\)

Activity 1

Single Positive Charge

  1. Reset the sheet.
  2. Add one positive hill near the center.
  3. Predict where \(\phi\), \(\rho\), \(\nabla^2\phi\), and \(E\) are largest.
Parameter Relationship Builder

How each parameter changes the Poisson result

  • Charge/source strength: larger \(|\rho|\) gives larger curvature and stronger potential response.
  • Sign: positive and negative sources bend the surface in opposite directions.
  • Permittivity/stiffness \(\epsilon\): larger \(\epsilon\) reduces curvature response for the same source.
  • Spread \(\sigma\): larger \(\sigma\) distributes the source over a wider region.
  • Position: moves the region where curvature is produced.

Students should express observations as proportional relationships before writing the full equation.

Activity 2

Parameter Sweep

Change only one slider at a time: \(\epsilon\), charge strength, or \(\sigma\). Students should explain the effect before asking AI.

Activity 3

Positive-Negative Dipole

  1. Use one positive and one negative charge.
  2. Look at the tilted potential landscape.
  3. Explain superposition and field direction.
Activity 4

Inspect Point Reasoning

Use Inspect Point Values near the charge center, on a slope, and far away.

Equation Establishment

From observation to Poisson equation

  1. Observe where the source \(\rho\) is located.
  2. Observe the local curvature \(\nabla^2\phi\).
  3. Test how changing \(\epsilon\) changes the curvature response.
  4. Write the relationship: curvature is proportional to source and inversely proportional to medium stiffness.
  5. State the equation: \(\nabla^2\phi=-\rho/\epsilon\).
